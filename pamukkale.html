Pamukkale

Pamukkale, meaning "Cotton Castle", is one of Turkey’s most breathtaking natural wonders. Famous for its dazzling white travertine terraces formed by mineral-rich thermal waters, it is also home to the ancient Greco-Roman city of Hierapolis.

Main Attractions

  • Thermal Pools & Travertine Terraces
  • Hierapolis Ancient Ruins
  • Cleopatra’s Pool
  • Pamukkale Antique Theatre
  • Atatürk Museum

Recommended Food

Enjoy local Turkish delicacies such as gözleme (stuffed flatbread), traditional Turkish tea, kebabs, and regional desserts.

Travel Tips

  • Wear non-slip footwear — the terraces can be slippery.
  • Visit early morning or near sunset for fewer crowds and beautiful lighting.
  • Combine your trip with a walk through the ancient ruins of Hierapolis.
